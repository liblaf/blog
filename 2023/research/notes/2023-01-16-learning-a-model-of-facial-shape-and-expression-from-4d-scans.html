    3. Model formulation

    FLAME is described by a function:

    M(β⃗,θ⃗,ψ⃗):R\absβ⃗×\absθ⃗×\absψ⃗→R3N M\pqty{\vec{\beta}, \vec{\theta}, \vec{\psi}}: \mathbb{R}^{\abs{\vec{\beta}} \times \abs{\vec{\theta}} \times \abs{\vec{\psi}}} \to \mathbb{R}^{3 N} M(β​,θ,ψ​):R\absβ​×\absθ×\absψ​→R3N

    • β⃗\vec{\beta}β​ — coefficients describing shape
    • θ⃗\vec{\theta}θ — coefficients describing pose
    • ψ⃗\vec{\psi}ψ​ — coefficients describing expression
    • \vbT‾∈R3N\overline{\vb{T}} \in \mathbb{R}^{3 N}\vbT​∈R3N — template mesh in the “zero pose” θ⃗∗\vec{\theta}^*θ∗
    • θ⃗∗\vec{\theta}^*θ∗ — “zero pose”
    • BS(β⃗;S):R\absβ⃗→R3NB_S\pqty{\vec{\beta}; \mathcal{S}}: \mathbb{R}^{\abs{\vec{\beta}}} \to \mathbb{R}^{3 N}BS​(β​;S):R\absβ​→R3N — shape blendshape function to account for identity related shape variation
    • BP(θ⃗;P):R\absθ⃗→R3NB_P\pqty{\vec{\theta}; \mathcal{P}}: \mathbb{R}^{\abs{\vec{\theta}}} \to \mathbb{R}^{3 N}BP​(θ;P):R\absθ→R3N — corrective pose blendshapes to correct pose deformations that cannot be explained solely by LBS
    • BE(ψ⃗;E):R\absψ⃗→R3NB_E\pqty{\vec{\psi}; \mathcal{E}}: \mathbb{R}^{\abs{\vec{\psi}}} \to \mathbb{R}^{3 N}BE​(ψ​;E):R\absψ​→R3N — expression blendshapes that capture facial expressions
    • W(\vbT‾,\vbJ,θ⃗,W)W\pqty{\overline{\vb{T}}, \vb{J}, \vec{\theta}, \mathcal{W}}W(\vbT​,\vbJ,θ,W) — A standard skinning function is applied to rotate the vertices of \vbT‾\overline{\vb{T}}\vbT​ around joints \vbJ∈R3K\vb{J} \in \mathbb{R}^{3 K}\vbJ∈R3K, linearly smoothed by blendweights W∈RK×N\mathcal{W} \in \mathbb{R}^{K \times N}W∈RK×N

    Model-only

    estimate the model coefficients \Bqtyβ⃗,θ⃗,ψ⃗\Bqty{\vec{\beta}, \vec{\theta}, \vec{\psi}}\Bqtyβ​,θ,ψ​ by optimizing

    E(β⃗,θ⃗,ψ⃗)=ED+λLEL+EP E\pqty{\vec{\beta}, \vec{\theta}, \vec{\psi}} = E_D + \lambda_L E_L + E_P E(β​,θ,ψ​)=ED​+λL​EL​+EP​

    ED=λD∑\vbvsρ(min⁡\vbvm∈M(β⃗,θ⃗,ψ⃗)\norm\vbvs−\vbvm) E_D = \lambda_D \sum_{\vb{v}_s} \rho \pqty{\min_{\vb{v}_m \in M\pqty{\vec{\beta}, \vec{\theta}, \vec{\psi}}} \norm{\vb{v}_s - \vb{v}_m}} ED​=λD​\vbvs​∑​ρ(\vbvm​∈M(β​,θ,ψ​)min​\norm\vbvs​−\vbvm​)

    • EDE_DED​ — measures the scan-to-mesh distance of the scan vertices \vbvs\vb{v}_s\vbvs​ and the closest point in the surface of the model
    • \vbvs\vb{v}_s\vbvs​ — scan vertices
    • λD\lambda_DλD​ — weight controls the influence of the data term
    • ρ\rhoρ — a Geman-McClure robust penalty function
    • ELE_LEL​ — a landmark term, measuring the L2-norm distance between image landmarks and corresponding vertices on the model template, projected into the image using the known camera calibration

    EP=λθ⃗Eθ⃗+λβ⃗Eβ⃗+λψ⃗Eψ⃗ E_P = \lambda_{\vec{\theta}} E_{\vec{\theta}} + \lambda_{\vec{\beta}} E_{\vec{\beta}} + \lambda_{\vec{\psi}} E_{\vec{\psi}} EP​=λθ​Eθ​+λβ​​Eβ​​+λψ​​Eψ​​

    • EPE_PEP​ — regularizes the pose coefficients θ⃗\vec{\theta}θ, shape coefficients β⃗\vec{\beta}β​, and expression coefficients ψ⃗\vec{\psi}ψ​ to be close to zero by penalizing their squared values

    6. Model training

    decouple shape, pose, and expression variations

    • \BqtyP,W,T\Bqty{\mathcal{P}, \mathcal{W}, \mathcal{T}}\BqtyP,W,T — pose parameters
    • E\mathcal{E}E — expression parameters
    • \Bqty\vbT‾,S\Bqty{\overline{\vb{T}}, \mathcal{S}}\Bqty\vbT​,S — shape parameters

    6.1. Pose parameter training

    • \vbTiP\vb{T}_i^P\vbTiP​ — personalized rest-pose templates
    • \vbJiP\vb{J}_i^P\vbJiP​ — person specific joints
    • W\mathcal{W}W — blendweights
    • P\mathcal{P}P — pose blendshapes
    • T\mathcal{T}T — joint regressor

    alternate between:

    • solve for the pose parameters θ⃗j\vec{\theta}_jθj​ of each registration jjj
    • optimize the subject specific parameters \Bqty\vbTiP,\vbJiP\Bqty{\vb{T}_i^P, \vb{J}_i^P}\Bqty\vbTiP​,\vbJiP​
    • optimize the global parameters \BqtyW,P,T\Bqty{\mathcal{W}, \mathcal{P}, \mathcal{T}}\BqtyW,P,T

    objective function being optimized consists of:

    • data term EDE_DED​ — penalizes the squared Euclidean reconstruction error of the training data
    • regularization term EPE_{\mathcal{P}}EP​ — penalizes the Frobenius norm of the pose blendshapes
    • regularization term EWE_{\mathcal{W}}EW​ — penalizes large deviations of the blendweights from their initialization

    To avoid \vbTiP\vb{T}_i^P\vbTiP​ and \vbJiP\vb{J}_i^P\vbJiP​ being affected by strong facial expressions, expression effects are removed when solving for \vbTiP\vb{T}_i^P\vbTiP​ and \vbJiP\vb{J}_i^P\vbJiP​. This is done by jointly solving for pose θ⃗\vec{\theta}θ and expression parameters ψ⃗\vec{\psi}ψ​ for each registration, subtracting BEB_EBE​ (Equation \eqrefeq:5\eqref{eq:5}\eqrefeq:5), and solving for \vbTiP\vb{T}_i^P\vbTiP​ and \vbJiP\vb{J}_i^P\vbJiP​ on those residuals.

    6.2. Expression parameter training

    • solve for the pose parameters θ⃗j\vec{\theta}_jθj​ of each registration
    • unpose: remove the pose influence by applying the inverse transformation entailed by M(0⃗,θ⃗,0⃗)M\pqty{\vec{0}, \vec{\theta}, \vec{0}}M(0,θ,0) (Equation \eqrefeq:1\eqref{eq:1}\eqrefeq:1)
    • \vbVjU\vb{V}_j^U\vbVjU​ — the vertices resulting from unposing the registration jjj
    • \vbViNE\vb{V}_i^{NE}\vbViNE​ — the vertices of the neutral expression of subject iii, also unposed
    • compute expression residuals \vbVjU−\vbVs(j)NE\vb{V}_j^U - \vb{V}_{s\pqty{j}}^{N E}\vbVjU​−\vbVs(j)NE​ for each registration jjj
    • s(j)s\pqty{j}s(j) — the subject index jjj
    • compute expression space E\mathcal{E}E by applying PCA

    6.3. Shape parameter training

    • \vbT‾\overline{\vb{T}}\vbT​ — computed as the mean of these expression- and pose-normalized registrations
    • S\mathcal{S}S — formed by the first β⃗\vec{\beta}β​ principal components computed using PCA

    6.4. Optimization structure

    Due to the high capacity and flexibility of the expression space formulation, pose blendshapes should be trained before expression parameters in order to avoid expression overfitting.
